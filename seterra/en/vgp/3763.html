Can you identify all 47 of these special Japanese cities? Each one is a prefectural capital, that is, a city where a prefectural government is located. Nagoya is the most populous, with over 2,300,000 people. Kyoto is one of the oldest cities in Japan, originally founded as Heian in 794. It's also Japan's third-largest city. Naha, the capital of Okinawa Prefecture, is located in an island group south of mainland Japan. By collecting facts about Japan and working with this map quiz, you can memorize the prefectural capitals of Japan with ease!
