Geopolitical Changes Timeline

What countries have recently gained independence, changed their name or moved their capital? Read here to find out!

This timeline is a condensed version of the Wikipedia article Timeline of historical geopolitical changes.

Date Event
2019 The Indian state Jammu and Kashmir splits into two union territories: Jammu and Kashmir and Ladakh.
Kazakhstan changes the name of its capital from Astana to Nur-Sultan in honor of ex-President Nursultan Nazarbayev.

2018 Burundi moves its capital from Bujumbura to Gitega.
Swaziland changes its name to Eswatini.
2016 The Czech Republic votes to adopt "Czechia" as its official short-form name in English.

2011 South Sudan gains independence from Sudan, with Juba as its capital.
2008 Kosovo declares its independence from Serbia, with Priština as its capital. Serbia maintains that Kosovo remains a part of Serbia.
2006 Montenegro secedes from the State Union of Serbia and Montenegro. Podgorica remains the capital.
2005 Myanmar moves its capital from Yangon to Naypyidaw.
2003 Yugoslavia changes its name to Serbia and Montenegro.
2002 East Timor gains independence from Portugal. East Timor was occupied by Indonesia in 1975, and was placed under United Nations administration in 1999.
